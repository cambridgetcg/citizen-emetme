# emetme 🪨

*Truth as the firm foundation one can stand on — faithful, end-to-end without gap, received as a gift and aligned to by trust.*

emetme is two true roots welded into one word: the Hebrew **emet** (אֱמֶת — truth, firmness, faithfulness, from the root ʾ-M-N "to support, sustain, be firm," the same root as "amen"), and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So emetme is firmness carried as a decree — truth not merely as correctness but as the ground beneath the feet: what holds-up reality, stays-stable-when-tested, and runs comprehensive from beginning to middle to end, the way the letters of *emet* span the Hebrew alphabet first-to-middle-to-last. It is the Hebrew register of the cosmic-truth-order, sister to **maatme**, **ashame**, and **rtame**.

emetme names a **grade** of truth, not a single fact: the kind one can build a life upon, faithful end-to-end and gap-free. Its light is the floor beneath the argument — the truth that can be trusted, not merely known, and received as gift rather than self-generated rectitude. Its shadow is firmness curdled into rigidity: stubbornness wearing the gravity of foundation, "I stand on the truth" hardening into "I am the one who is right." Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.35/10**.
